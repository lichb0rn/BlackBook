# [[Marcus Aurelius and pain]]

Although [[Marcus Aurelius|Marcus]] was a [[Stoicism|Stoic]], he also drew inspiration from another, more surprising source when it came to coping with pain and illness: the rival philosophical school of [[Epicurus]]. The [[Epicureans]] believed that the goal of life was pleasure (hedone). They described pleasure, though, in a notoriously paradoxical manner, as consisting mainly of a state of freedom from pain and suffering (ataraxia). Minimizing the emotional distress caused by pain and illness was therefore extremely important to them.

Marcus quotes this letter and then exhorts himself always to act as Epicurus did: remain focused on the pursuit of wisdom even in the face of illness, pain, or any other hardship.

**Our main concern should always remain the use we are making right now, from moment to moment, of our own mind.**
