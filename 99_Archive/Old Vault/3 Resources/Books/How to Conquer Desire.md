The Greek word for joy (chara) is closely related to that for gratitude (charis). Indeed, the Stoics encourage you **to appreciate the external things Fortune has given you**. ## STEPS FOR CHANGING DESIRES

You should carefully evaluate your habits and desires in terms of the bigger picture: **how much do these pursuits actually contribute to your long-term happiness or sense of fulfillment in life?**

A simple framework for evaluating and changing your behavior based on a combination of [[Cognitive Behavioral Therapy|cognitive therapy]] and ancient Stoic practices. It consists of the following steps:

1.‚ÄÖEvaluate the consequences of your habits or desires in order to select which ones to change.
2.‚ÄÖSpot early warning signs so that you can nip problematic desires in the bud.
3.‚ÄÖGain cognitive distance by separating your impressions from external reality.
4.‚ÄÖ**Do something else instead of engaging in the habit.**

1.‚ÄÖPlanning new activities that are consistent with your core values.
2.‚ÄÖContemplating the qualities you admire in other people.
3.‚ÄÖPracticing gratitude for the things you already have in life.


### EVALUATING THE CONSEQUENCES OF DESIRES

You may find it also helps to picture the positive consequences of refraining from the desire, mastering it, or doing the opposite of it.
